# Style Guide — Instagram "Beautiful"

The Instagram look: **premium, clinical, editorial.** Where TikTok grabs attention, Instagram earns trust. Every IG post should look like a page from a research lab's field notes — calm, precise, and obviously credible. Reference: [[POST-20260603-001]] (`_assets/POST-20260603-001-ig-01.png` … `-03.png`).

## Why this style
The IG audience and the research buyer reward authority and restraint. Clean type, generous space, a single accent colour and a visible "we test everything" standard do more for conversion than colour or hype. This is the format that gets **saved** and that makes the brand look bigger than it is.

## The format
- **Carousel of 3–5 slides**, 1080 × 1350 each.
- **Slide 1 — Cover:** kicker label + big serif headline (the question/claim) + one-line subhead + a small line-art vial + `swipe →`.
- **Middle slides:** one idea each. A numbered section label, a short serif statement, a small diagram or a tight paragraph. Never a wall of text.
- **Last slide — Trust + CTA:** the payoff, then **THE INDEXA STANDARD** card ("Every compound ships with a third-party COA. Verify the batch before you trust the vial."), then a CTA ("Read the full primer → link in bio").

## Visual system (design tokens)
- **Paper** `#F4F1E9` (warm off-white background)
- **Ink** `#16181D` (headlines, key text)
- **Indigo** `#3D34C9` (the single accent — labels, highlights, one diagram colour)
- **Sub** `#5B5C57` (body / secondary)
- **Hairline** `#CFC9B8` (the thin frame + rules)
- **Type:** serif for headlines (editorial weight) + clean sans for body + mono, letter-spaced, ALL-CAPS for kicker/footer labels. This three-voice system *is* the brand on IG.
- **Frame:** a 2px hairline inset border on every slide; a header rule under `INDEXA / RESEARCH NOTES / NN/03` and a footer rule above `@indexalabs` + disclaimer. Consistency across slides = the "system" feeling.
- **Imagery:** thin line-art (vials, bead chains, molecules) in ink + indigo. No stock photos, no gradients, no drop shadows.

## Layout rules
- One idea per slide. Lots of breathing room; let the paper show.
- Left-aligned text, consistent left margin (~84px inside the frame).
- Headline ≤ 6 words; subhead ≤ 12. If it doesn't fit on two lines, cut words.
- Always: page number (NN/03), `@indexalabs`, and the research-use disclaimer in the footer.

## Copy rules
- Calm, exact, no hype. "Studied across…", "what research used…", never "boosts/cures".
- The cover headline is usually a question or a flat true statement.
- Caption: expand the carousel into a clean paragraph; lead with the hook line; end with a save prompt + disclaimer. 8–12 researched hashtags.

## Hard rules (guardrail)
- Research/educational framing only. **No dosing, no medical claims.** Disclaimer in footer of slides **and** in caption.
- The COA/Verify trust line should appear on the final slide of most posts — it's our strongest conversion asset.

## Reusable template (SVG generator)
The three slides are generated by `mockups/gen_ig.py` (in the session outputs) and stored as editable `.svg` in `_assets/`. To make a new carousel: duplicate a slide SVG, change the kicker/headline/body, keep the frame + tokens, then export:
```bash
convert -density 96 -background none POST-YYYYMMDD-NNN-ig-01.svg POST-YYYYMMDD-NNN-ig-01.png
```
(Canva alternative: rebuild this token set as a brand kit — same colours, serif + mono pairing, hairline frame — for faster non-code editing.)
