# Retatrutide TRIUMPH-1: up to 28.3% weight loss in pivotal Phase 3 (2026)

> [!info] Provenance: Phase 3 RCT topline (sponsor announcement, broadly corroborated)
> Eli Lilly topline release for **TRIUMPH-1**, announced **21 May 2026**, corroborated by Healio, AJMC, Pharmacy Times, TCTMD and BioPharm International. Topline tier — full peer-reviewed publication pending. Retatrutide is a **first-in-class GIP + GLP-1 + glucagon triple receptor agonist** and a flagship catalog product.

## Key findings
- **TRIUMPH-1** (Phase 3; adults with obesity/overweight + ≥1 weight-related comorbidity, **without diabetes**): at **80 weeks**, average weight loss **19.0% (4 mg)**, **25.9% (9 mg)**, **28.3% (12 mg)** vs **2.2% placebo** (treatment-regimen estimand; efficacy estimand up to ~**30.3%**). **45.3%** of 12 mg patients lost **≥30%** of body weight — a level long associated with bariatric surgery (~70.3 lb at 12 mg).
- **TRIUMPH-4** (first Phase 3 readout, **Dec 2025**): up to **28.7%** weight loss (~32.3 kg / 71.2 lb) at 68 weeks, **75.8% reduction in osteoarthritis pain**, ~**20% LDL** reduction, **72% reversal** of prediabetes to normoglycemia.
- Further readouts due **later in 2026**: **TRIUMPH-2** (obesity + type 2 diabetes) and **TRIUMPH-3** (established cardiovascular disease).
- Tolerability: dose-dependent **GI adverse events** (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea); some analysts flagged the AE profile.

## Why it matters for this base
- Existing KB evidence for [[Retatrutide]] was **Phase 2** (Jastreboff 2023, ~24% at 48 wk) plus transcripts. This is the **first Phase 3 confirmation**, and the first to add **osteoarthritis pain** and **cardiometabolic** endpoints — the strongest GLP-class weight-loss data to date.
- Directly strengthens the Fat Loss / Metabolic cluster and retatrutide's positioning vs tirzepatide/semaglutide; the glucagon arm ([[Glucagon Agonism]] → [[Lipolysis]]/energy expenditure) is the differentiator.

## Caveats
- **Topline / press-release tier** — full peer-reviewed data pending; treatment-regimen vs efficacy estimands differ; GI AEs are dose-dependent. Investigational; **not FDA-approved**. Research/educational use only.
