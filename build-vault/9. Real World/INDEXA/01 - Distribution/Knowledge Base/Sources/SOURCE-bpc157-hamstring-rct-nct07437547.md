# First registered RCT of BPC-157: SubQ vs placebo for acute hamstring strain (NCT07437547)

> [!info] Provenance: registered interventional RCT (recruiting — no results yet)
> ClinicalTrials.gov record **NCT07437547**. Page is JS-rendered; details below are from the search-engine record and corroborated by a 2026 peer-reviewed narrative review. This is the **first properly registered randomized, placebo-controlled trial of BPC-157 for a musculoskeletal indication** — a meaningful credibility step, but it is *recruiting*, not reported.

## Key findings
- **NCT07437547** — randomized, placebo-controlled trial: **subcutaneous BPC 157 once daily for 14 days** vs **matching placebo** in adults with **acute hamstring muscle strain**, with tissue-repair endpoints.
- Context (peer-reviewed): the 2026 narrative review *"Regeneration or Risk? A Narrative Review of BPC-157 for Musculoskeletal Healing"* (PMC12446177) confirms human evidence remains **3 small uncontrolled pilot studies** (interstitial cystitis; IV safety/PK; retrospective intra-articular knee) — this RCT is the first controlled human test.

## Why it matters for this base
- [[BPC-157]] is a flagship healing product whose **#1 weakness is human evidence** (animal-only since ~1993; the KB note flags only "3 tiny studies" and a "US phase-2 hamstring trial reportedly underway"). This **upgrades that anecdote to a registered RCT** and is exactly the kind of data a serious customer asks for.
- If it reads out **positive**, this becomes **P0 news** for the Healing & Recovery cluster.

## Caveats
- **Recruiting / registered only — no results.** Registration ≠ efficacy or safety conclusion. BPC-157 remains **not FDA-approved**; research/educational use only. Tumor-growth caution (VEGF upregulation) unchanged.
