# Oral semaglutide misses in Phase 3 Alzheimer's trials (EVOKE / EVOKE+)

> [!info] Provenance: two Phase 3 RCTs — peer-reviewed, **NEGATIVE** primary result
> **EVOKE** and **EVOKE+** topline reported by Novo Nordisk, presented at CTAD 2025 / AD-PD 2026, and **published in The Lancet (2026)**. High confidence — large, double-blind, placebo-controlled, peer-reviewed. This is a **negative** finding and is recorded for honest evidence-tiering.

## Key findings
- **EVOKE + EVOKE+** — two randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase 3 trials; **3,808 adults aged 55–85** with mild cognitive impairment or mild dementia due to Alzheimer's disease, **confirmed amyloid positivity**; **104-week** treatment phase (+ planned 52-week extension). Intervention: **oral semaglutide 14 mg** (flexible dose).
- **Primary endpoint — change from baseline in CDR-SB (Clinical Dementia Rating–Sum of Boxes) at week 104: NO difference vs placebo.** Secondary **ADCS-ADL-MCI** (function) also showed no difference.
- **Biomarkers:** semaglutide produced a **~30% reduction in CRP** and other inflammation markers — but this did **not** translate into any cognitive or functional benefit.
- Safety/tolerability consistent with prior semaglutide. The **1-year extension was discontinued** after the null primary readout.

## Why it matters for this base
- [[Semaglutide]] carries a widely-cited observational **"−45% dementia risk"** longevity claim in this KB (see the Semaglutide and [[GLP-1 Agonism]] notes). **EVOKE/EVOKE+ is the definitive interventional test of that hypothesis in symptomatic early AD — and it is negative.** The epidemiological dementia-risk association did **not** replicate as a treatment effect. This directly **tempers the "GLP-1 for brain / longevity" angle** and is exactly the clinical-vs-observational split the Research-Literacy pillar exists to teach.
- For [[Neuroprotection]]: the largest, cleanest GLP-1 neuro-outcome trial to date is null — a caution against over-reading mechanistic anti-inflammatory signals as clinical benefit.

## Caveats
- **Null in symptomatic early AD ≠ no role in prevention or other populations.** The separate observational *prevention* signal (lower dementia *incidence* in cohorts) is a different question from *treating* established disease. Only **oral 14 mg** was tested; injectable/higher doses were not. Experts (ADDF) note the biomarker (CRP) engagement without clinical benefit may point toward combination-therapy directions. Not a treatment recommendation; research/educational use only.
