# Sauna

## What it is
- Passive **heat exposure**. Main types: **traditional/Finnish dry** (high heat, low humidity), **steam** (lower heat, high humidity), and **infrared/near-infrared** (lower air temp, radiant heating deeper into tissue).

## How it works
- Heat stress → **heat-shock proteins**, improved cardiovascular/endothelial function (NO), increased blood flow, and a transient **GH** rise; a hormetic stressor ([[Mitohormesis]]). Near-infrared also synergises with red-light/[[GHK-Cu]] for skin.

## Goal relevance
- Recovery (blood flow, relaxation), cardiovascular longevity, and tissue **healing** (pairs with repair peptides via increased perfusion).

## How to do it
- Common protocol ~**15–20 min, 80–100°C dry (or per device), 2–4×/week**; hydrate + electrolytes. Evening sauna can aid sleep onset (body-temp rebound).

## Cautions
- Dehydration, blood-pressure drops, not immediately post-resistance-training if hypertrophy is the goal (heat/blood-flow may blunt some adaptation — minor).
