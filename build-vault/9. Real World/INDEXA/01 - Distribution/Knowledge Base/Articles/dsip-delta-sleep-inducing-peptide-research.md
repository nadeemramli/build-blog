# DSIP: Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide Research

*Research on the neuropeptide for sleep, stress, and recovery optimization*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/dsip-delta-sleep-inducing-peptide-research
> **Abstract:** Delta Sleep-Inducing Peptide (DSIP) is a neuropeptide originally isolated for its sleep-promoting effects. Research has expanded to include stress modulation, pain management, and hormone regulation.

## 1. Overview

DSIP (Trp-Ala-Gly-Gly-Asp-Ala-Ser-Gly-Glu) is a nine-amino acid neuropeptide first isolated from rabbit brain in 1977. Named for its ability to induce delta wave (slow-wave) sleep, subsequent research has revealed broader neuromodulatory effects.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 Sleep Architecture**
DSIP promotes slow-wave (delta) sleep, the most restorative phase of the sleep cycle.

**2.2 Stress Response**
Modulates the HPA axis, potentially reducing stress hormone release.

**2.3 Endorphin Release**
May stimulate endogenous opioid peptide release.

**2.4 LH Release**
Research suggests effects on luteinizing hormone and gonadal function.

## 3. Research Applications

**3.1 Sleep Disorders**
Research on insomnia, sleep architecture disruption, and jet lag.

**3.2 Chronic Stress**
Studies on stress-related conditions and adrenal function.

**3.3 Pain Research**
Investigation of analgesic properties through endorphin modulation.

**3.4 Withdrawal Studies**
Research on substance withdrawal and recovery support.

## 4. Research Protocols

**4.1 Available Sizes**
DSIP is available in 10mg vials.

**4.2 Timing**
Research typically involves evening administration aligned with natural sleep onset.

**4.3 Blood-Brain Barrier**
DSIP crosses the blood-brain barrier, allowing peripheral administration.
