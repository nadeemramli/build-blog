# SS-31 (Elamipretide): Mitochondrial Protector

*Research on the cardiolipin-targeting peptide for cellular energy optimization*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/ss-31-elamipretide-mitochondrial-protection
> **Abstract:** SS-31 (Elamipretide) is a cell-permeable peptide that targets the inner mitochondrial membrane, specifically cardiolipin. It represents a unique approach to supporting mitochondrial function and cellular energy production.

## 1. Overview

SS-31 (D-Arg-Dmt-Lys-Phe-NH2), also known as Elamipretide or Bendavia, is a tetrapeptide that selectively concentrates in mitochondria and binds to cardiolipin, a phospholipid essential for electron transport chain function. This unique targeting makes it a powerful tool for mitochondrial research.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 Cardiolipin Protection**
SS-31 stabilizes cardiolipin, preventing its oxidation and maintaining electron transport chain efficiency.

**2.2 ROS Reduction**
By optimizing electron flow, SS-31 reduces mitochondrial reactive oxygen species production.

**2.3 ATP Enhancement**
Improved electron transport leads to more efficient ATP production.

**2.4 Mitochondrial Dynamics**
Research suggests effects on mitochondrial fusion/fission balance.

## 3. Clinical Research

**3.1 Heart Failure Studies**
Phase 2 trials have examined SS-31 in primary mitochondrial myopathy and heart failure.

**3.2 Ischemia-Reperfusion**
Research on protection during cardiac surgery and ischemic events.

**3.3 Age-Related Decline**
Studies on mitochondrial dysfunction in aging.

**3.4 Kidney Function**
Research on renal ischemia and function.

## 4. Research Protocols

**4.1 Available Sizes**
SS-31 is available in 10mg vials.

**4.2 Cell Permeability**
SS-31 rapidly enters cells without requiring transporters.

**4.3 Dosing Considerations**
Research uses various routes including subcutaneous and intravenous administration.
