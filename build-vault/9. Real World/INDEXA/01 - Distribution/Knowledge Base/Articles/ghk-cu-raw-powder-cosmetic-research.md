# GHK-Cu Raw Powder: Copper Peptide Research for Skin Regeneration

*Comprehensive guide to GHK-Cu powder applications in cosmetic and dermatological research*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/ghk-cu-raw-powder-cosmetic-research
> **Abstract:** GHK-Cu (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine Copper) is a naturally occurring copper complex with extensive research supporting its role in skin remodeling, wound healing, and anti-aging applications. This guide covers bulk powder formulation considerations.

## 1. Overview & Background

**1.1 What is GHK-Cu?**
GHK-Cu is a tripeptide-copper complex naturally present in human plasma, saliva, and urine. Discovered in 1973, it has become one of the most studied peptides in dermatological research.

**1.2 Natural Occurrence**
Plasma levels decline from ~200 ng/mL at age 20 to ~80 ng/mL by age 60, correlating with reduced regenerative capacity.

**1.3 Bulk Powder Advantages**
Raw powder form allows researchers to create custom formulations, control concentrations, and integrate into various delivery systems.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 Collagen & ECM Remodeling**
Stimulates synthesis of collagen I, III, and elastin while modulating matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) for balanced ECM turnover.

**2.2 Copper Delivery**
The copper ion is essential for lysyl oxidase activity, critical for collagen and elastin cross-linking.

**2.3 Gene Expression**
Studies show GHK-Cu modulates expression of 4,000+ genes, with notable upregulation of tissue repair genes and downregulation of inflammatory genes.

**2.4 Antioxidant Properties**
Demonstrates significant free radical scavenging and protection against lipid peroxidation.

## 3. Research Applications

**3.1 Anti-Aging Formulations**
Research focuses on reducing fine lines, improving skin firmness, and restoring skin density.

**3.2 Wound Healing**
Accelerates wound contraction, angiogenesis, and epithelialization in preclinical models.

**3.3 Hair Follicle Research**
Studies indicate potential for enlarging hair follicles and stimulating hair growth.

**3.4 Scar Remodeling**
Investigation into reducing hypertrophic scarring and improving scar appearance.

## 4. Formulation Considerations

**4.1 Stability**
GHK-Cu is relatively stable but should be protected from oxidation. Store powder desiccated at -20°C.

**4.2 Solubility**
Freely soluble in water. Typical research concentrations range from 0.1% to 1%.

**4.3 pH Sensitivity**
Optimal stability at pH 5.5-6.5. Copper may dissociate at extreme pH values.

**4.4 Compatibility**
Compatible with most cosmetic ingredients. Avoid strong chelating agents that may strip copper.
