# MOTS-c: The Mitochondrial-Derived Metabolic Peptide

*Research on the exercise mimetic peptide for metabolic optimization*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/mots-c-mitochondrial-metabolic-peptide
> **Abstract:** MOTS-c is a mitochondrial-derived peptide that acts as a powerful metabolic regulator. Often called an "exercise mimetic," it represents a new frontier in longevity and metabolic health research.

## 1. Introduction

MOTS-c (Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the Twelve S rRNA-c) is a 16-amino acid peptide encoded in the mitochondrial genome. Discovered in 2015, it represents a new class of signaling molecules that communicate mitochondrial status to the rest of the cell and body.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 AMPK Activation**
MOTS-c activates AMP-activated protein kinase, the master metabolic regulator, mimicking effects of exercise and caloric restriction.

**2.2 Folate-Methionine Cycle**
The peptide modulates one-carbon metabolism, affecting cellular methylation and energy production.

**2.3 Glucose Homeostasis**
Research shows improved insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake in skeletal muscle.

**2.4 Exercise Mimetic**
MOTS-c replicates many beneficial effects of physical exercise at the molecular level.

## 3. Research Applications

**3.1 Metabolic Health**
Studies on obesity, insulin resistance, and metabolic syndrome.

**3.2 Aging Research**
MOTS-c levels decline with age, making supplementation a focus of longevity research.

**3.3 Exercise Physiology**
Understanding exercise benefits and enhancing physical performance.

**3.4 Mitochondrial Function**
Research on mitochondrial health and cellular energy production.

## 4. Research Protocols

**4.1 Available Sizes**
MOTS-c is available in 10mg vials for research applications.

**4.2 Administration**
Most research uses subcutaneous or intravenous administration.

**4.3 Storage**
Highly stable as lyophilized powder; store at -20°C for long-term stability.
