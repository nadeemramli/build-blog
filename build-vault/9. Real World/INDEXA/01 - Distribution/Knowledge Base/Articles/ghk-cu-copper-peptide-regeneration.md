# GHK-Cu: Copper Peptide for Tissue Regeneration

*Comprehensive research guide on the multi-functional copper-binding tripeptide*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/ghk-cu-copper-peptide-regeneration
> **Abstract:** GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper-binding peptide with remarkable regenerative properties. This guide covers its diverse mechanisms from collagen synthesis to gene expression modulation.

## 1. Introduction

GHK-Cu (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine-Copper) is a naturally occurring tripeptide-copper complex found in human plasma, saliva, and urine. Discovered in the 1970s, it has been extensively researched for wound healing, anti-aging, and regenerative applications. Available in both injectable (100mg vials) and topical powder (1g) forms.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 Collagen Synthesis**
GHK-Cu stimulates collagen I, III, and elastin production, improving tissue structure and elasticity.

**2.2 Gene Expression Modulation**
Remarkably, GHK-Cu affects the expression of over 4,000 human genes, generally resetting gene expression toward a healthier pattern.

**2.3 Anti-Inflammatory**
Reduces inflammatory markers and modulates the inflammatory response during healing.

**2.4 Antioxidant Effects**
Provides protection against oxidative damage and supports tissue integrity.

## 3. Research Applications

**3.1 Skin Regeneration**
Extensive research on wound healing, wrinkle reduction, and skin rejuvenation.

**3.2 Hair Follicle Health**
Studies show effects on hair thickness and follicle size.

**3.3 Bone & Cartilage**
Research on bone regeneration and cartilage repair.

**3.4 Systemic Effects**
Systemic administration research explores broader regenerative applications.

## 4. Available Forms

**4.1 Injectable (100mg)**
Higher concentration for systemic research applications.

**4.2 Powder Form (1g)**
For topical formulation research and larger-scale applications.

**4.3 Copper Importance**
The copper ion is essential for biological activity; the tripeptide without copper is inactive.

## 5. Safety Profile

**5.1 Natural Occurrence**
As an endogenous compound, GHK-Cu has an excellent safety profile.

**5.2 Copper Considerations**
Copper supplementation considerations apply with high-dose or prolonged use.

**5.3 Topical Safety**
Extensive use in cosmetic applications demonstrates topical safety.
