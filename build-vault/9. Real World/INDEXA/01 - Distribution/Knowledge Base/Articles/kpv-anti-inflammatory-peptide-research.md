# KPV: Potent Anti-Inflammatory Tripeptide

*Research on the alpha-MSH derived peptide for inflammation and gut health*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/kpv-anti-inflammatory-peptide-research
> **Abstract:** KPV (Lys-Pro-Val) is a tripeptide derived from alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH) that retains potent anti-inflammatory activity without melanotropic effects. This guide covers its unique properties and research applications.

## 1. Overview

KPV represents the C-terminal tripeptide of α-MSH responsible for the hormone's anti-inflammatory effects. By isolating this fragment, researchers can study anti-inflammatory mechanisms without the tanning and appetite effects of full α-MSH.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 NF-κB Inhibition**
KPV directly inhibits NF-κB nuclear translocation, reducing inflammatory gene transcription.

**2.2 Cytokine Modulation**
Reduces production of pro-inflammatory cytokines including TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6.

**2.3 Cell Penetration**
Uniquely, KPV can enter cells directly, providing intracellular anti-inflammatory action.

**2.4 Gut Barrier**
Research shows particular efficacy in intestinal inflammation and barrier function.

## 3. Research Applications

**3.1 Inflammatory Bowel Disease**
Extensive research in colitis and Crohn's disease models.

**3.2 Skin Inflammation**
Studies on dermatitis, psoriasis, and inflammatory skin conditions.

**3.3 Wound Healing**
Anti-inflammatory support for tissue repair research.

**3.4 Systemic Inflammation**
Research on systemic inflammatory conditions.

## 4. Research Protocols

**4.1 Available Sizes**
KPV is available in 10mg vials.

**4.2 Oral Stability**
Unlike most peptides, KPV shows activity with oral administration, particularly for gut applications.

**4.3 Combination Use**
Often combined with tissue repair peptides for comprehensive healing protocols.
