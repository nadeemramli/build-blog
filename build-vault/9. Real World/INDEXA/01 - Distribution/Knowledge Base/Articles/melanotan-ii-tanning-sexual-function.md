# Melanotan II: Tanning & Sexual Function Research

*Research on the melanocortin peptide with multiple physiological effects*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/melanotan-ii-tanning-sexual-function
> **Abstract:** Melanotan II is a synthetic melanocortin analog that produces both melanogenic (tanning) and sexual function effects. This guide covers its dual mechanisms and research applications.

## 1. Introduction

Melanotan II (MT-II) is a cyclic lactam analog of alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone (α-MSH) developed at the University of Arizona. It activates melanocortin receptors producing both skin pigmentation and sexual arousal effects, leading to the development of PT-141 as a more selective sexual function peptide.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 MC1 Receptor (Skin)**
Activation of MC1R on melanocytes stimulates eumelanin production, causing skin darkening.

**2.2 MC3/MC4 Receptors (CNS)**
Central melanocortin activation produces sexual arousal and appetite suppression.

**2.3 UV-Independent Tanning**
MT-II can induce pigmentation without UV exposure, though UV enhances the effect.

**2.4 Appetite Effects**
MC4R activation may reduce appetite and food intake.

## 3. Research Applications

**3.1 Photoprotection Research**
Studies on protective tanning for UV-sensitive individuals.

**3.2 Sexual Function**
Research on libido and arousal mechanisms.

**3.3 Obesity Research**
Studies on melanocortin-based appetite regulation.

**3.4 Skin Disorders**
Research on conditions like erythropoietic protoporphyria.

## 4. Research Considerations

**4.1 Available Sizes**
MT-II is available in 10mg vials.

**4.2 Side Effects**
Common effects include nausea, flushing, and fatigue in research settings.

**4.3 Mole Monitoring**
Research protocols should include monitoring of existing nevi.
