# HCG: Human Chorionic Gonadotropin Research

*Research on the gonadotropin for fertility and hormone optimization*

> **Source:** https://indexalabs.com/blog/hcg-fertility-hormone-research
> **Abstract:** Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (HCG) is a glycoprotein hormone with applications in fertility research and testosterone restoration studies. This guide covers its mechanisms and research applications.

## 1. Introduction

Human Chorionic Gonadotropin (HCG) is a hormone produced during pregnancy that shares structural similarity with luteinizing hormone (LH). This allows it to stimulate testosterone production and is used in fertility research and hormone optimization studies.

## 2. Mechanisms of Action

**2.1 LH Mimicry**
HCG binds to and activates LH receptors, stimulating Leydig cells to produce testosterone.

**2.2 Testicular Function**
Maintains testicular size and function during conditions that suppress natural LH.

**2.3 Spermatogenesis**
Supports sperm production through intratesticular testosterone maintenance.

**2.4 Ovulation Induction**
In female research, triggers ovulation similar to the natural LH surge.

## 3. Research Applications

**3.1 Male Fertility**
Studies on hypogonadism, infertility, and testosterone restoration.

**3.2 TRT Adjunct**
Research on maintaining fertility during testosterone replacement therapy.

**3.3 Female Fertility**
Ovulation induction research in assisted reproduction.

**3.4 Endocrine Studies**
Fundamental research on gonadal function and regulation.

## 4. Research Protocols

**4.1 Available Sizes**
HCG is available in 5000 IU vials.

**4.2 Reconstitution**
Reconstitute with bacteriostatic water; use within 60 days when refrigerated.

**4.3 Storage**
Lyophilized HCG is stable at room temperature; reconstituted must be refrigerated.
