# Cagrilintide

## Overview
- `[educational]` A long-acting **amylin analog** (often paired with semaglutide, e.g. CagriSema) for weight management. — [[TRANSCRIPT-huberman-peptides-solo|Huberman]]

## Mechanisms
- `[educational]` [[Amylin Agonism]] — slows gastric emptying and enhances satiety, complementary to the appetite/insulin effects of [[GLP-1 Agonism]]; the rationale for pairing it with semaglutide.

## Evidence
- `[literature]` **Phase 3 REDEFINE-1/-2 (ADA 2025 / NEJM):** as **CagriSema** (cagrilintide 2.4 mg + semaglutide 2.4 mg, once-weekly), **20.4% weight loss** at 68 wk in adults without diabetes (22.7% full-adherence; 40.4% ≥25%) and **13.7%** in type 2 diabetes — beating semaglutide alone (14.9%) and cagrilintide alone (11.5%). First-in-class amylin/GLP-1 combo; **NDA filed Dec 2025, FDA decision expected late 2026**. Upgrades Cagrilintide's evidence from tier-C anecdote to a pivotal Phase 3. — [[SOURCE-cagrisema-redefine-phase3-2026]]
