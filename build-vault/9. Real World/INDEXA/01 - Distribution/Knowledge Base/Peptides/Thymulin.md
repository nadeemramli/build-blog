# Thymulin

## Overview
- `[educational]` The **9-amino-acid, zinc-dependent** thymic peptide (the French-isolated "true" main thymus hormone) — the **marker of thymus function**, falling with age. Distinct from the Russian polypeptide mix "thymalin". — [[TRANSCRIPT-huberman-bakri-peptides|Bakri]]

## Mechanisms
- `[educational]` Develops NK/T-cells and, in animal data, **sensitises end-organs to their target hormone** — e.g. [[HCG]] + thymulin yields *more* testosterone than hCG alone (a thymus↔reproduction link). — [[TRANSCRIPT-huberman-bakri-peptides|Bakri]]

## Evidence
- `[educational]` Has a very short half-life → the practical goal is raising **endogenous** thymulin, which requires **adequate zinc** (falling thymulin is the *earliest* sign of zinc depletion, before serum/RBC zinc). — [[TRANSCRIPT-huberman-bakri-peptides|Bakri]]
