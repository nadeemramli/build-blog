## Evidence




- `[educational]` Longevity angle: [[MOTS-C]] 'trains' mitochondria via AMPK, [[SS-31]] gives structural (cardiolipin) support, and GLP-1 agonists also improve mito function/↓oxidative stress — all targeting mitochondrial dysfunction as a hallmark of aging. — [[TRANSCRIPT-top5-longevity-peptides|Longevity Top-5]]

- `[educational]` [[MOTS-C]] drives the **AMPK → SIRT1 → PGC-1α** axis (mito biogenesis, fat/glucose handling) and inhibits **NF-κB** — the molecular basis of **mitohormesis** ('exercise in a vial'); it favors endurance (slow-twitch) adaptations. — [[TRANSCRIPT-mots-c-deep-dive|MOTS-C deep dive]]

- `[educational]` Two complementary mito strategies: [[SS-31]] *stabilizes structure* (cardiolipin/ETC → ↑ATP, ↓ROS) while [[MOTS-C]] *signals adaptation* (AMPK/mitohormesis). SS-31 carries no overstress risk; MOTS-C does. — [[TRANSCRIPT-ss31-deep-dive|SS-31 deep dive]]

- `[literature]` **First registered human RCT of a mitochondrial-derived peptide (2026):** [[MOTS-C]] Phase 2a NCT07505745 tests OGTT-derived insulin sensitivity (Matsuda Index) in prediabetes + overweight/obesity — the AMPK/mitohormesis thesis moving from preclinical into a controlled human metabolic endpoint. Recruiting, no results. — [[SOURCE-motsc-prediabetes-phase2a-nct07505745]]
