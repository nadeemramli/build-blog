## What it is
- `[educational]` Agonism of the **amylin receptor** — amylin is a β-cell peptide co-secreted with insulin that **slows gastric emptying, suppresses glucagon, and enhances satiety**. Long-acting analogs ([[Cagrilintide]]) add a satiety mechanism **complementary to** [[GLP-1 Agonism]], the basis for GLP-1 + amylin combinations.

## Peptides acting on it
- [[Cagrilintide]] (long-acting amylin analog; paired with semaglutide as CagriSema).

## Evidence
- `[literature]` **CagriSema Phase 3 (REDEFINE-1/-2, ADA 2025 / NEJM):** adding amylin agonism (cagrilintide 2.4 mg) to GLP-1 (semaglutide 2.4 mg) raised weight loss to **20.4%** at 68 wk (vs 14.9% for semaglutide alone; 11.5% for cagrilintide alone) in adults without diabetes, and **13.7%** in type 2 diabetes — the first pivotal readout quantifying the amylin arm's additive effect. — [[SOURCE-cagrisema-redefine-phase3-2026]]
