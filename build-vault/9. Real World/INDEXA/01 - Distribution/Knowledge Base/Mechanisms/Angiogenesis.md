## Evidence



- `[educational]` GHK-Cu promotes angiogenesis via **VEGF/FGF-2** upregulation (supplying skin/hair/tissue with nutrients for repair) — a shared mechanism with BPC-157, and a reason for the same cancer-caution framing. — [[TRANSCRIPT-ghk-cu-deep-dive|GHK-Cu deep dive]]

- `[educational]` TB4 promotes angiogenesis via actin-driven endothelial migration — **less potent than BPC-157's** NO/VEGF route, but complementary (why the two are combined for major injuries); also a shared cancer-caution mechanism. — [[TRANSCRIPT-tb4-tb500-deep-dive|TB4/TB500 deep dive]]

- `[literature]` **First human RCTs of both angiogenic repair peptides registered (2026):** BPC-157 SubQ for acute hamstring strain ([[SOURCE-bpc157-hamstring-rct-nct07437547]]) and a TB-500 (TB4 17–23) Phase 1/2 in stable ASCVD ([[SOURCE-tb500-ascvd-rct-nct07487363]]) — both recruiting, no results yet.
