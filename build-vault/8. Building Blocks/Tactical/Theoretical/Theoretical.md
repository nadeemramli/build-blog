# Theoretical

> [!summary]
> Tactics we have **observed or collected** but not yet proven. This is the fast, cheap, untrusted shelf of the [[Tactical]] layer — a place to dump ideas, swipes, and hypotheses the moment you see them, before they're lost. Nothing here is trustworthy until it has been built and executed.

## What belongs here

Anything you notice and want to keep: a competitor's move, a creator's hook formula, a pricing idea, a supplier tactic, a thread you read. Capture it fast and loose:

- **The observation** — what you saw, with a source link if you have one.
- **The hypothesis** — why it might work for us.
- **Cost to test** — a rough sense of effort to validate.

> [!caution] Untrusted by default
> A Theoretical note is *not a recommendation*. It's a candidate. Do not act on it as if it were proven — that's what the [[Practical]] shelf is for. Capturing freely is the point; the discipline is keeping it quarantined until tested.

## The promotion path

```
observe / collect  →  Theoretical (this shelf)  →  build & execute  →  Practical
   (fast, cheap)         (untrusted)               (the gate)         (trusted)
```

A tactic graduates **Theoretical → [[Practical]]** only by being built and run. On promotion, move the note into the matching Practical domain and rewrite it as documentation (what happened) rather than speculation (what might). Ideas that get tested and fail can be deleted or archived with a one-line postmortem.
