# Market

> [!summary]
> The demand side of the business — everything to do with creating, deserving, and capturing demand. Market is one of the three top-level entities ([[Three Entities|Market · Capital · Operation]]). It contains three pillars: **Branding**, **Product**, and **Distribution (Growth)**. What was originally modelled as a standalone "Distribution" entity is actually the *Growth* pillar sitting inside this larger umbrella.

## Why Market is the umbrella (the model upgrade)

The first version of this vault treated **Capital · Operation · Distribution** as the triad, and folded product into Operation's inventory. That under-served the most important question for a course business or personal brand: *what is the product, and why does anyone want it?*

The corrected model promotes the triad to **Market · Capital · Operation** (the Commoncog "three sisters" of business power). Distribution doesn't disappear — it becomes one of three things Market owns:

```
                         MARKET  (own the demand)
        ┌────────────────────┼─────────────────────┐
   [[Branding]]          [[Product]]          [[Distribution]]
   Moats · Advantage     Value Architecture    Growth:
   · Power               · JTBD · Value Prop    acquisition →
   (why you win &        · Demand               conversion →
    keep winning)        (why it's wanted)      retention
```

## The three pillars

| Pillar | Owns | Core question |
|---|---|---|
| **[[Branding]]** | Moats, advantage, power | *Why do you win, and keep winning?* |
| **[[Product]]** | Value architecture, JTBD, value proposition, demand | *Why does anyone want this?* |
| **[[Distribution]]** | Acquisition, conversion, retention | *How does it reach and stick to people?* |

> [!note] The order matters
> Branding and Product define *what deserves to grow*; Distribution is *how it grows*. Pouring Distribution effort into a product with no real demand (weak Product) or no durable edge (weak Branding) is the classic growth trap. Get Product and Branding honest first, then let Distribution compound them.

## How Market connects to the other entities

- **← [[Capital]]**: funds the demand engine (ad budget, product development) and is constrained by it (runway caps spend). Capital can also be *wielded* as a Market weapon — see [[Capital#Capital expertise: raise, allocate, wield]].
- **← [[Operation]]**: makes and delivers what Product promises; Market's demand signal tells Operation what to build/stock.
- **↑ [[Governing Artifacts]]**: brand guideline, market/competitor intel, and the driver tree all feed the Market pillars.

## KPIs roll-up
Each pillar has its own KPIs that decompose through the [[Governing Artifacts#4. Driver tree / value architecture|driver tree]]:
- Branding → share of search, brand recall, pricing power
- Product → activation, value realized, demand signals
- Distribution → CAC, conversion %, retention/LTV
